A Tradition Unlike Any Other

The Masters Tournament, first played in 1934, is the first major championship of the year and the only major that's played at the same course each year. The pristine Augusta National Golf Club, co-founded by Bobby Jones and Clifford Roberts, has become synonymous with excellence in golf.

Tournament Traditions

  • The Green Jacket, awarded to the champion since 1949
  • The Champions Dinner, held Tuesday of tournament week
  • The Par 3 Contest, played on Wednesday
  • The Honorary Starters ceremony opening the tournament

The Course

Length 7,510 Yards
Par 72
Surface Bentgrass

Augusta National is known for its pristine conditions and challenging holes, including the famed Amen Corner (holes 11, 12, and 13) and the dramatic finishing hole, the par-4 18th, named "Holly."
